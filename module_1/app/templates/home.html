Hi, I'm Jonah Thomas.

Signal Processing Engineer at Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory

I am a Signal Processing Engineer at Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory (APL), currently participating in the Discovery Program, a rotational initiative designed to develop future technical leaders across complex national security, space, defense, healthcare, cyber, and applied research problem spaces.

My background spans statistical and analytical signal processing, navigation systems, hardware design, and system-level testing, giving me a strong foundation in both the theory and implementation of sensing, estimation, and autonomous systems.

I am particularly interested in the intersection of guidance, navigation, and control; perception; applied machine learning; and sensor fusion, especially as artificial intelligence becomes increasingly integrated into physical systems across defense, aerospace, autonomy, robotics, and intelligent platforms.

To complement my work at APL, I am pursuing a second master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence from Johns Hopkins University. My goal is to build on my signal processing and navigation background by developing deeper expertise in machine learning, computer vision, intelligent systems, and data-driven approaches to perception and autonomy.

Long term, I hope to contribute to the development and integration of advanced AI-enabled sensing, navigation, and autonomous capabilities for high-impact mission areas. I am especially motivated by work that connects deep technical understanding with systems-level thinking: translating algorithms, sensor data, and mission needs into robust capabilities for real-world environments.
